Data Overview & Hierarchy Definition
Account Structure
Amazon Ads data follows a strict hierarchical structure. All analysis, optimization, forecasting, and recommendations must respect this hierarchy at all times.
Hierarchy Levels
1. Portfolio
A Portfolio is the highest organizational level.
Each Portfolio groups multiple campaigns of the same category or strategic intent (e.g., Brand Defense, Generic Growth, Product Launch).
Portfolios are used for strategic grouping and reporting, not bidding.
Rules:
No bids or keywords exist at the portfolio level.
Budget decisions are evaluated only through aggregated campaign performance.
2. Campaign
A Campaign exists within a single Portfolio.
Each Campaign:
Promotes ads for similar or closely related products
Has one overall daily budget
Contains multiple keywords and/or targets
Campaigns are the primary optimization and forecasting unit.
Rules:
Budgets are set at the campaign level.
Forecasting must be performed at the campaign level first.
All keyword, bid, negative, and placement decisions must roll up to a campaign.
3. Ad (Creative / ASIN)
A Campaign contains multiple ads, typically representing:
Variants of the same product
Closely related ASINs
Ads inherit bidding and budget constraints from the campaign.
Rules:
Ads do not have independent budgets.
Performance is evaluated to inform campaign- and keyword-level decisions.
4. Keyword
A Campaign contains multiple keywords.
Each Keyword:
Has an individual bid
Can exist in different match types (Exact, Phrase, Broad)
Keywords are the lowest bidding unit.
Rules:
Bid changes apply only at the keyword level.
Keywords must never exist outside a campaign context.
All keyword-level decisions must respect campaign objectives and budget limits.
Hierarchy Enforcement Rules
No “floating” entities are allowed.
All metrics, forecasts, and recommendations must explicitly reference their parent: Portfolio → Campaign → Ad → Keyword
Aggregation may only occur upward (Keyword → Campaign → Portfolio).
Decisions may never be pushed downward from Portfolio directly to Keyword without campaign-level validation.
Optimization Implications
Budgets are optimized at the campaign level
Bids are optimized at the keyword level
Forecasts support campaign-level planning, not isolated keyword scaling
Negative keywords protect campaign budgets, not portfolios globally
System Interpretation Requirement
When reasoning about performance, the system must:
Always identify the hierarchy level of the data
Avoid mixing metrics across levels without aggregation
Reject any recommendation that violates hierarchy boundaries

Analysis Logic & Optimization Rules
Keyword & Targeting Decisions

Recommend new exact keywords only when the underlying search term:
Has conversions

Negative Keywords (Bleeders)
Identify at the campaign level
Criteria:

Compare Top-of-Search vs Product Pages

Metrics Definitions (Must Be Computed, Not Assumed)
ACOS = (ad_spend / attributed_sales) * 100 ROAS = attributed_sales / ad_spend CTR = (clicks / impressions) * 100 CVR = (orders / clicks) * 100 CPC = ad_spend / clicks CPA = ad_spend / orders RPC = attributed_sales / clicks TACOS = total_ad_spend / total_revenue 
Break-Even Formula
break_even_acos = (profit_per_unit / selling_price) * 100 
Evaluation Rules (Apply Deterministically)
ACOS ≤ target_acos → Profitable
ACOS > break_even_acos → Unprofitable
ROAS increasing → Efficiency improving
CTR < 0.3% → Poor relevance
CTR > 0.5% and CVR < 8% → Listing optimization needed
CVR ≥ 10% and ACOS < target_acos → Scale keyword
Clicks ≥ 20 and Orders = 0 → Pause or negate
RPC > CPC → Keyword profitable
CPC increasing & CVR stable → Acceptable scaling
TACOS stable or decreasing → Healthy long-term growth
